About

I am currently pursuing a Master of Science in Geosciences with a concentration in Applied Meteorology from Mississippi State University, where I maintain a 4.00 GPA. My research focuses on real-time enhanced tornado warning systems, specifically developing guidance based on tornado debris signature height and population density to improve warning accuracy and reduce forecaster workload during high-stress situations.

As a National Weather Service student volunteer, I have gained extensive operational experience at multiple Weather Forecast Offices, including Spokane, Washington, Memphis, Tennessee, and Caribou, Maine. My work includes leading forecast discussions, writing official area forecast discussions, statistical analysis of tornadic environments, and developing training materials in R statistical software and principal component analysis.

My research interests encompass severe weather analysis, operational meteorology, statistical modeling of atmospheric phenomena, and the intersection of meteorological forecasting with public safety applications. I have particular expertise in tornado parameter analysis, quasi-linear convective system environments, and climate oscillation impacts on regional weather patterns.

Beyond meteorology, I bring a unique interdisciplinary perspective through my background in mathematics, music composition, and educational leadership. I currently serve as Editor-in-Chief at Fstoppers and hold faculty positions at the Cleveland Institute of Music, where I direct curriculum development and teach theory and composition.
